Work Experience at SiLab

Silab is a maker space on campus designed to promote student personal, club and class projects. The maker space has a wide variety of machines that support working with wood, plastic, metal and textiles. Silab is open everyday and offers students the safety training and project advice needed to move their specific project forward. The maker space also runs community events like competitions and workshops.

Work Description

I led safety trainings to teach the Boston University College of Engineering community how to use makerspace tools safely and efficiently. I also organized and ran educational workshops on CAD in SolidWorks, 3D printing, fabric manipulation, and sewing. In addition, I supported students with both class and personal projects by explaining the capabilities of lab equipment and sharing my own hands-on experience.
