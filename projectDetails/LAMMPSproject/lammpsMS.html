Molecular Dynamics Simulations of Surface Melting in FCC, BCC, and HCP Metals: Influence of Surface Orientation and System Size on Melting Temperature and Diffusion Properties

This project explored how atomic structure and crystallographic orientation influence melting behavior in metals using molecular dynamics (MD) simulations.

← Back to Projects

Project Overview

I investigated phase transitions in three metals with different crystal structures: aluminum (face-centered cubic, FCC), tungsten (body-centered cubic, BCC), and titanium (hexagonal close-packed, HCP). For each material, simulations were performed on multiple crystallographic planes to examine how atomic arrangement affects surface dynamics and melting behavior. In cubic systems, the [100], [110], and [111] planes were analyzed, while for the HCP structure, the basal [0001], prismatic [1100], and pyramidal [0111] planes were examined using the HCP four-axis coordinate system.

Below you can see the testing planes chosen in each metal.

Through these simulations, I was able to compare melting behavior across crystal structures and orientations, gaining insight into how atomic-scale geometry influences material properties. This project strengthened my understanding of computational materials science, crystallography, and phase transitions, while highlighting the power of molecular dynamics as a tool for studying complex physical phenomena.
